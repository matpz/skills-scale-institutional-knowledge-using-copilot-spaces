# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA/Testing Lead

### Role Summary
QA/Testing Leads define quality standards, develop test strategies, and ensure features meet acceptance criteria before release. They collaborate with Product and Development teams to balance speed with quality.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain test plans aligned with acceptance criteria
- Design and execute manual and automated test scenarios
- Track and triage bugs and defects
- Define quality gates and Definition of Done (DoD) testing requirements
- Coordinate load/performance testing when applicable
- Provide feedback on testability during design reviews
- Collaborate with developers on test automation and CI/CD integration

### Goals
- Catch defects early and reduce production issues
- Enable confident, rapid releases
- Improve product stability and user experience
- Establish and maintain quality benchmarks

### Typical Communication
- QA planning during sprint kickoff
- Bug reports and defect tracking via issue tracking system
- Test result summaries in status updates
- Quality metrics and trend reporting

---

## Design/UX Lead

### Role Summary
Design/UX Leads own user experience decisions, design systems, and usability validation. They ensure products are intuitive, accessible, and aligned with brand standards.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- Maintain design system and component library
- Participate in design reviews and provide feedback to stakeholders
- Advocate for accessibility and inclusive design principles
- Collaborate with Product Managers on feature prioritization based on user needs
- Review implementations for design compliance and user experience

### Goals
- Deliver delightful, accessible user experiences
- Reduce user friction and support burden
- Maintain design consistency across products
- Improve user adoption and satisfaction

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews during planning and development phases
- Usability feedback and research findings
- Design system updates and documentation
- Accessibility guidance and design patterns

---

## Security Engineer

### Role Summary
Security Engineers embed security practices into the development lifecycle, conduct threat assessments, and ensure compliance with security policies and regulations.

### Responsibilities
- Perform threat modeling and security reviews during design phase
- Conduct code and architecture security assessments
- Manage security scanning and vulnerability remediation workflows
- Define security requirements and acceptance criteria for features
- Coordinate incident response and post-incident reviews
- Maintain compliance with relevant standards (e.g., GDPR, SOC 2, HIPAA)
- Provide security training and guidance to development teams

### Goals
- Reduce security vulnerabilities and risk exposure
- Shift security left into design and development phases
- Maintain regulatory and compliance posture
- Build a culture of security awareness across the organization

### Typical Communication
- Security reviews during design phase
- Vulnerability reports and remediation tracking
- Security incident escalations and response coordination
- Compliance and audit communications

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge business needs and technical implementation. They gather requirements, document processes, and validate that solutions deliver business value.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct stakeholder interviews and requirements gathering
- Document business processes, workflows, and use cases
- Create requirements traceability matrices
- Validate solutions against business objectives and success metrics
- Identify and escalate scope changes and impacts
- Support User Acceptance Testing (UAT) coordination and sign-off
- Facilitate communication between business stakeholders and technical teams

### Goals
- Ensure alignment between business needs and technical delivery
- Reduce scope creep and rework
- Improve stakeholder satisfaction and adoption
- Enable data-driven decision making on prioritization

### Typical Communication
- Requirements documentation and review sessions
- Stakeholder interviews and business requirement updates
- UAT coordination and issue escalation
- Business impact analysis and trade-off discussions

---

## Scrum Master / Agile Coach

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile ceremonies, remove impediments, and coach teams on iterative delivery practices. They foster continuous improvement and team collaboration.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint planning, daily standups, sprint reviews, and retrospectives
- Remove blockers and escalate impediments that hinder team progress
- Coach team members on agile practices, ceremonies, and principles
- Track and report on team velocity, burndown, and delivery metrics
- Support process improvements based on retrospective feedback
- Mentor junior team members on agile discipline and collaboration
- Protect the team from external distractions and scope creep

### Goals
- Maximize team velocity and predictability
- Foster psychological safety and continuous learning
- Maintain sprint discipline and iterative delivery cadence
- Build high-performing, self-organizing teams

### Typical Communication
- Facilitation of all agile ceremonies and meetings
- Team coaching and impediment escalation
- Metrics and velocity reporting to stakeholders
- Process improvement recommendations and action tracking
